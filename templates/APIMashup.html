Playing with Code: Films and Music

Introduction

These are two projects done with Python. The first one explores the relationship between the dominating gender in a film and its plot. The second one yields a list of the most popular tracks of the 10 artists who are liked most on the SoundCloud Facebook page.

  • Tools

    Python / Natural Language Toolkit / REST API / JSON / BeautifulSoup / SQL

  • Timeline

    September 2016 - December 2016; February 2017

Movie Plot and Gender (Ongoing)

Datasets and Method

I got the inspiration for this project from a data visualization project here. My first dataset comes from the Github repository of this project, and it contains the main characters and their genders of 2000 films. I used BeautifulSoup to parse the IMDB pages of these 2000 films, and got their plot keywords and other metadata.

When at least 60% of the main characters in a film are female, we consider the film “woman’s film”. When at least 60% of the main characters in a film are male, we consider the film “man’s film”. Then we explore the relationship between the dominating gender in a film and its plot keywords.

Data visualization

105 out of the 2000 films turn out to be women’s films. I tried using different criteria to determine the “gender” of films, and the results are the same! On one hand, "Women’s film" is a niche genre that is specifically about the life experience of women, especially the social, emotioanl aspect of it. One the other hand, men’s films are most films, full of thrills brought by classic plots such as murder and revenge.

The wordclouds raised many new questions. Why is New York City this important for both men and women? What are the plots of the men's films tagged with "female protagonist"? Are films with "female protagonist" about women or men? I will further explore these questions with other data including the number of words spoken by each character. I will also explore the relationship among gender, plot, and other metadata such as rating and country.

I am also interested in how the trend of popular plots change over time, so I tried creating area charts of the most popular plot keywords. It shows that death is a persistent theme in main-stream films. More data is needed to create a more complete visualization of plot trends, especially for women's films.
