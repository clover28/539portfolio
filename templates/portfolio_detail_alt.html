Overcoming Various Constraints

Like every other product, this app needs to realize the goals of clients, meet the needs of users, and overcome the constraints of the physical exhibition and technologies.

  • Our faculty clients:
    • Want the app to navigate visitors through the exhibition
    • Want the app to be both appealing to the general public and educational
    • Want the possibility of extending it as a general tool for the education of Buddhist culture
  • The pysical environment:
    • Requires our app to map to the physical exhibition
  • Most importantly, our users told us that they would like:
    • Information beyond the exhibit
    • Narration and guide through the exhibit
    • Reward system for using
    • Interface that is easy to use
  • And their pain points are...
    • Buddhism is oftentimes not a familiar or attractive topic for them
    • Worried that technology is distracting instead of helpful in a physical exhibition

We aligned users' needs and our design ideas with the Value Proposition Canvas. By discussing our Value Proposition Canvas with our clients, we helped them prioritize their goals, and determined that the main goal of the product should be enhancing the experience of those who visit the museum. The free app will contain audio guide and detailed information to relieve users' doubt about the necessity of a museum app. It will leverage the unique content and enable users to experience the journey of Hyecho, a young Korean Monk in the 8th century, in order to raise people's interest in the Buddhist content.
